Publications

  • On the Benefits of Heterogeneity in Cognitive Stability and Flexibility for Collaborative Task Switching

    Alessandra Brondetta, Anastasia Bizyaeva, Maxime Lucas, Giovanni Petri, Sebastian Musslick

    Paper CogSci 2024

    We model how the trade-off between cognitive stability and flexibility shapes group task-switching. In our multi-agent simulations, heterogeneous teams (mix of more stable and more flexible thinkers) outperform homogeneous ones in fast-changing environments—especially when the most flexible members handle switch cues. The findings inform how to compose teams and design supports in educational and clinical settings.

  • Not Yet AlphaFold for the Mind: Evaluating Centaur as a Synthetic Participant

    Sabrina Namazova, Alessandra Brondetta, Younes Strittmatter, Matthew Nassar, Sebastian Musslick

    Paper

    Simulators speed science by generating realistic data—think AlphaFold for protein structures. Behavioral science seeks a comparable participant simulator that produces human-like behavior across tasks. We review Centaur (an LLM fine-tuned on 160 experiments) against core criteria: it predicts well, but its generative behavior diverges from human data. Takeaway: a strong direction with clear potential, but additional development is required to reach participant-simulator reliability and cognitive-model completeness.

  • Mentevo: A Python Library for Cognitive Stability and Flexibility Simulation

    Alessandra Brondetta

    Github

    Mentevo is a compact Python library for simulating and analyzing a dynamical system model of cognitive stability and flexibility in task-switching environments, within groups of agents. Originally developed for the study presented in Brondetta et al. (2024), it provides tools to explore how environmental and task conditions (e.g., task-switching rates) affect individual and group task-switching performance. The library also includes parametrization options, performance metrics and visualization methods.
